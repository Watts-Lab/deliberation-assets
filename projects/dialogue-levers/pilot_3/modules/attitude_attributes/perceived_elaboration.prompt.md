notes: |
  Construct: Perceived (Prior) Elaboration — "the extent to which an
  individual has carefully scrutinized and thought about the merits of
  information relevant to the attitude object" (Wegener et al. 1995).

  This item taps prior elaboration (Format 1 in the README's source
  list) — "how much have you thought about this issue?" — rather than
  in-task elaboration (Format 2). Prior elaboration is what we want:
  it serves as a substitute for "I haven't thought much about this"
  options that we excluded from the attitude scale itself, per
  Krosnick et al. (2002).

  Wording from Luttrell et al. (2016), adapted to "this topic" for
  generality across the issues in the study.

  See README.md > Perceived Elaboration for the full trail.
