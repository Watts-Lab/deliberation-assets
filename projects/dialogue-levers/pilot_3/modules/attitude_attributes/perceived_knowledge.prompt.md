type: multipleChoice
notes: |
  Construct: Perceived Knowledge — "the information stored in memory
  regarding an attitude object" (Wegener et al. 1995; Visser et al.
  2006). We use a self-report of knowledge about the specific issue
  (rather than political knowledge generally) so the measure is
  comparable across topics.

  Self-assessed knowledge is used in preference to a fact-quiz approach:
  Boudreau & Lupia (2011) argue that small fact-based question sets
  poorly generalize to "citizen competence." Self-assessments correlate
  with motivation and learning satisfaction (Sitzmann et al. 2010);
  this is an acceptable target for a meta-attitudinal instrument.

  See README.md > Perceived Knowledge for the full trail.
