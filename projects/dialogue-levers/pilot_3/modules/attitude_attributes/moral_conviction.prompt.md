notes: |
  Construct: Moral Conviction — "a meta-cognition about an attitude —
  specifically, one's belief that a given attitude is a reflection of
  one's core feelings or beliefs about fundamental right and wrong"
  (Skitka et al., via Wisneski et al. 2020).

  Wording adapted from Ryan (2014) / Wisneski et al. (2020). We
  rewrote scale points to avoid the awkward "Much / Very Much"
  pairing common in source instruments. Other operationalizations
  considered (Format 2: "reflection of core moral beliefs", Format 3:
  "a moral stance", Format 4: "based on strong moral principles") are
  documented in the README — researchers using a composite Moral
  Conviction Index may want to include several of these formats and
  average them.

  See README.md > Moral Conviction for the full trail.

### How strongly is your opinion on this topic connected to your moral beliefs about right and wrong?

---
- 0: Not connected at all
- 0.25: Weakly connected
- 0.5: Moderately connected
- 0.75: Strongly connected
- 1: Completely connected
