---
type: multipleChoice
notes: |
  Construct: Ambivalence — "the degree to which a person has both
  favorable and unfavorable reactions to an object … evaluative
  conflict in a person's responses to an object" (Thompson, Zanna, &
  Griffin 1995, via Visser et al. 2006).

  This item operationalizes the cognitive (mixed) basis rather than
  the affective (conflict) basis. The wording is adapted from
  Tourangeau, Rasinski, Bradburn & D'Andrade (1989): "Would you say
  that you are strongly on one side or the other on the X issue, or
  would you say your feelings are mixed?"

  Note that ambivalence is distinct from "no strong opinion" — an
  ambivalent participant has weighed strong arguments on both sides,
  not failed to engage with the question.

  See README.md > Ambivalence for the full trail (including
  indecision and conflict format variants).

### Would you say that you are strongly on one side of this topic, or that your feelings are mixed?

---
- 0: No mixed feelings at all
- 0.25: Slightly mixed feelings
- 0.5: Moderately mixed feelings
- 0.75: A lot of mixed feelings
- 1: Extremely mixed feelings
